About Us

Who Are We

Mercy Hands Domiciliary Care Ltd offers individualized, dedicated, personal and comprehensive homecare service. We deliver care that is tailored and made with the patient at the centre of all decision making and care planning. This philosophy ensures personalised care for positive patient outcomes as partners in care. Personalised and positive patient care is cascaded within our organisation and embedded in all our colleagues through training, supervision and appraisal.

Our Objectives

  1. To promote excellence in patient care and professional care practice through encouragement, promotion and support of professional development and engagement.
  2. To continue with the practice development, while working with our patients, their families and cares, involving them in decision making about their care and treatment, geared to individual needs while encouraging them to become involved in decisions relating to care delivery.
  3. To always show our patients courtesy and respect irrespective of ethnic origin, religious belief, personal attributes or the nature of the health problem.
  4. To respond positively to feedback from our patients, their family’s, other health and social care professionals and our colleagues.
  5. Evaluate the performance of our team and making recommendations for merit awards and personnel classification changes. Counselling and disciplining colleagues to enable achievement of our objectives, particularly as related to high-quality patient care.
  6. To manage the care service efficiently and effectively to make the best use of resources and to maximise value for money for our patients.

Our Aim

We comply with and exceed all regulatory and statutory requirements and standards, which will be achieved through the core objectives that we have identified. This will be done by;

  1. Providing holistic care needs assessment and management of services users’ needs and risks through comprehensive care planning, networking and specific interventions as prescribed by other professionals that are suited to individual needs, diversity and risks.
  2. Developing a community where anyone needing our services or support will feel safe and confident in the services we provide and above all, express their satisfaction about the overall services received. All colleagues will share this belief and will be supported to ensure that our mission is reflected in every aspect of the services we provide.
  3. Working autonomously with the service user and all that are involved in their care. This way of work aims to promote, improve, maintain and restore health. We will safeguard the privacy and confidentiality of our patients and enable them to maintain their independence, have informed choices and opportunity to enjoy and contribute to society.
  4. Ensuring the right staff skill mixes are available to patients’ needs by investing in staff development to provide a progressive and expert workforce. We value each of our colleagues equally for their expertise and experience they bring to the team and will provide our colleagues with a working environment within which effective teamwork, where appropriate, and personal and group development are actively encouraged and supported.
  5. Having a management that is committed to the continual mentored training and development of staff in line with the organization’s overall aim of improving quality service and efficiency.
  6. Processing of complaints through the appropriate procedure. Our internal recruitment and selection criteria are designed to enable us to satisfy industry standards and regulatory requirements. Our rigorous compliance to procedure, which includes a face-to-face interview, Certificate of Good Conduct, evidence of relevant training, professional registration and at least two satisfactory references means that we can select quality staff for all assignments.

Our team

  • The team members allocated to support specific patients will be chosen to match skills with needs and minimize travelling distances to support good time attendance.
  • Staffing levels may be changed at the discretion of the Manager dependent on the needs.
  • We manage and train our employees with the aim that all our cares achieve Personal Care Assistants Certification. All other employees receive the training appropriate to their work.
  • All employees will receive annual training in health and safety matters such as moving and handling, fire awareness and procedures, adult protection issues and a range of other health and environmental matters.
  • We have a dedicated recruitment consultant in each sector we serve, specializing in the divisions of temporary and permanent staff, both regional and nationally.
