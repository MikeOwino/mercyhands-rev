Our Services

Mercy Hands Domiciliary Care provides a range of high quality domiciliary care services including;

  • Assistance help - getting up, wash, shower or bath, dressing and having breakfast.
  • Shopping support - alongside our customer or alternatively we can come in, agree a shopping list, go to the shops, come back and put it all away.
  • Preparation of food and drink.
  • Ensuring a timely prompt for medication when due or medication reminders.
  • Undertaking domestic tasks such as cleaning, laundry and ironing either on a regular basis or as a ‘spring clean’, whatever the season.
  • Provision of a respite service. If performing the role of principal care, support will be provided during time off each week.
  • Recreational activities.
  • Pampering sessions – the occasional visit to organise bathing, clean clothes, fresh bedding and a thoughtfully prepared meal before bedtime.
  • At the end of each day, some help with getting ready for bed. Once we have considered the sort of care our customer needs, a member of our team will discuss the customers individual requirements in more detail.

Our Target Market

  • Our targeted age groups are adults between 18 and 65years and Adults aged 65 years and above.
  • Long Term Conditions and Terminally Ill Patients

Far too many times, people with chronic illness and long-term conditions find themselves pigeonholed, often losing their identity, when in fact, each is an individual. Sometimes care will be of a practical nature, help with getting up, washing or dressing or perhaps help with meals, laundry and ironing. But it may be about going out to the shops, or walk in the park or a visit relatives or friends.

Many people with chronic or long term conditions are looked after on a day to day basis by a spouse or other family member. But caring relatives need a break from time to time and this is where our ‘sitting service’ can help. On an arranged day each week we will come and take over the role - allowing the family member time to themselves. Some of our customers may require live-in care, which is also available.

Details of the patients care is provided within the individualized plan. In essence we will cover patients below;

  • The elderly
  • Dementia & Alzheimer’s patients
  • Patients who have been discharged from hospital
  • Infirm
  • Patients needing companionship or help with everyday tasks
  • Patients with learning difficulties or autistic spectrum
  • Patients who are disabled or immobile and are at home
  • Patients requiring palliative at home
  • Patients with disorder sensory impairment
