Who Are We

Mercy Hands Domiciliary Care offers individualized, dedicated, personal and comprehensive homecare service. We deliver care that is tailored and made with the patient at the centre of all decision making and care planning. This philosophy ensures personalised care for positive patient outcomes as partners in care. Personalised and positive patient care is cascaded within our organisation and embedded in all our colleagues through training, supervision and appraisal.

Providing holistic care needs assessment and management of care services tailored to our individual patient’s needs.

Developing a community where anyone needing our services or support will feel safe.

Working autonomously with the service user and all that are involved in their care.

Our Company Overview

We provide world class domiciliary care services to the growth market across Kenya.

The size of market and opportunity is vast. Kenya is underserved in the domiciliary care sector.

Choosing a care provider is very different as it is based on the patients’ personal criteria. The standards, level of reporting, assurances about care and simple quality are all factors that are taken with deep consideration.

Our ability to show care time on site, track visit times, advise on care whereabouts, consistency with who the care is and commitment to training and development puts Mercy Hands Domiciliary Ltd in a class of its own.

This model has proven successful in a more competitive and crowded marketplace. Using in a less crowded and more opportune market becomes ideal.

Our Target Market

  1. Our targeted age groups are adults between 18 and 65years and Adults aged 65 years and above.
  2. Long Term Conditions and Terminally Ill Patients

Far too many times, people with chronic illness and long-term conditions find themselves pigeonholed, often losing their identity, when in fact, each is an individual. Sometimes care will be of a practical nature, help with getting up, washing or dressing or perhaps help with meals, laundry and ironing. But it may be about going out to the shops, or walk in the park or a visit relatives or friends.


Many people with chronic or long term conditions are looked after on a day to day basis by a spouse or other family member. But caring relatives need a break from time to time and this is where our ‘sitting service’ can help. On an arranged day each week we will come and take over the role - allowing the family member time to themselves. Some of our customers may require live-in care, which is also available.


Details of the patients care is provided within the individualized plan. In essence we will cover patients below;

  • The elderly
  • Dementia & Alzheimer’s patients
  • Patients who have been discharged from hospital
  • Infirm
  • Patients needing companionship or help with everyday tasks
  • Patients with learning difficulties or autistic spectrum
  • Patients who are disabled or immobile and are at home
  • Patients requiring palliative at home
  • Patients with disorder sensory impairment
